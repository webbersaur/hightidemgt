High Tide Commodities Management provides computer disposal, IT asset disposition, and electronic recycling for Hamden, Connecticut businesses. Hamden's Diverse Business Base

Hamden carries a longer industrial history than most New Haven County towns — and that legacy still shows up in the local economy. The Whitney Avenue and Dixwell corridors host manufacturers, fabricators, and distribution operations, many of which run substantial IT infrastructure. Quinnipiac University's three Hamden campuses generate steady refresh cycles for classrooms, dorms, faculty offices, and research labs. The growing biotech and professional services presence around the I-91 interchange has added another wave of clients who need reliable end-of-life IT handling.

Why Hamden Businesses Choose High Tide

Bulk-friendly. Manufacturing and university clients often have volume — boxes of laptops from a department refresh, pallets of monitors from a building consolidation. We handle bulk pickup without the fees and surcharges national providers tack on for "non-standard" loads.

Quinnipiac-area familiarity. Higher-education IT refreshes have specific quirks: end-of-semester timing, student data on devices, asset-tag accountability, and mixed-condition inventory. We've handled the workflow before and can move quickly without surprises.

Manufacturing-comfortable. Industrial IT — ruggedized PCs, embedded systems, control room equipment — often contains proprietary data and unusual storage media. We're set up to identify, document, and properly destroy what's on it.

R2 certified. Every monitor, server, and component is processed under the R2v3 standard. No informal export, no landfill. More on R2 certification.
